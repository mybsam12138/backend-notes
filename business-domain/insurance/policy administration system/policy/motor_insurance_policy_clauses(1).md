# Insurance Policy Clauses (Motor Insurance Example)

## What is a Policy Clause

A **policy clause** is a specific provision or contractual condition
written in an insurance policy.\
It explains how the insurance coverage works, what is covered, what is
excluded, and how claims are handled.

A policy is essentially composed of many clauses that define the rights
and obligations of the insurer and the insured.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Example: Motor Insurance Policy Clauses

Below are common clauses that appear in a **motor insurance policy**.

    Motor Insurance Policy
    │
    ├── Coverage Clause
    ├── Exclusion Clause
    ├── Conditions Clause
    ├── Deductible Clause
    ├── Territorial Clause
    └── Claims Clause

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 1. Coverage Clause

The **coverage clause** defines what risks or losses are insured.

### Example

    The insurer shall indemnify the insured for accidental loss or damage
    to the insured vehicle caused by collision, fire, theft, or natural disasters.

Meaning: The insurance company will compensate the insured if the
vehicle is damaged due to covered events.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 2. Exclusion Clause

The **exclusion clause** specifies situations where the insurer will not
provide coverage.

### Example

    The insurer shall not be liable for any loss or damage occurring
    while the insured vehicle is used for illegal racing or unlawful activities.

Meaning: If the vehicle is used for illegal activities, the insurer will
not pay for the loss.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 3. Conditions Clause

The **conditions clause** describes obligations that the insured must
follow.

### Example

    The insured must maintain the insured vehicle in good condition
    and comply with all applicable traffic laws.

Meaning: If the insured violates the policy conditions, the insurer may
deny a claim.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 4. Deductible Clause

The **deductible clause** specifies the amount the insured must pay
before the insurer pays the rest.

### Example

    A deductible of USD 500 shall apply to each claim for damage to the insured vehicle.

Example calculation:

Repair cost: \$2000\
Deductible: \$500

Insurer pays: \$1500\
Insured pays: \$500

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 5. Territorial Clause

The **territorial clause** defines the geographical area where the
insurance coverage applies.

### Example

    This policy provides coverage only within mainland China.

Meaning: If an accident occurs outside the covered territory, the policy
will not apply.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 6. Claims Clause

The **claims clause** explains how claims must be reported and
processed.

### Example

    The insured must notify the insurer within 48 hours after the occurrence
    of any accident or loss and provide all necessary documentation.

Meaning: If the insured fails to report the accident within the required
time, the claim may be rejected.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Summary

A motor insurance policy is composed of multiple clauses, each defining
a specific rule.

  Clause Type          Purpose
  -------------------- -------------------------------------------------
  Coverage Clause      Defines what risks are covered
  Exclusion Clause     Defines situations not covered
  Conditions Clause    Specifies obligations of the insured
  Deductible Clause    Defines the amount the insured must pay first
  Territorial Clause   Defines where the coverage applies
  Claims Clause        Defines how claims must be reported and handled
