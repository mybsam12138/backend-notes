# Invoices in the Tax System

## Purpose of Invoices
Invoices are **legal tax documents** used to:
- Confirm that a transaction occurred
- Identify seller and buyer
- Support tax reporting and audit
- Substantiate revenue or expenses

---

## Types of Invoices

### VAT Special Invoice
- Displays:
  - Net amount
  - VAT rate
  - VAT amount
- Allows **input VAT deduction**
- Typically used for **B2B transactions**

### VAT Ordinary Invoice (Paper or Electronic)
- Usually shows **tax-inclusive amount only**
- Cannot be used for VAT deduction
- Common in consumer transactions

---

## Electronic vs Paper Invoices

| Aspect | Electronic Invoice | Paper Invoice |
|---|---|---|
| Legal validity | Equal | Equal |
| Tax authority record | Yes | Yes |
| Verification | QR / Code | Manual / Code |
| Trend | Mainstream | Declining |

---

## Invoice and Tax Authority Systems
- Issued through **tax-controlled systems**
- Each invoice has:
  - Unique invoice code
  - Unique invoice number
- Invoice data is uploaded to the **national tax database**
- Authenticity can be verified via official platforms

---

## Invoices and Tax Treatment

### For Sellers
- Issued invoices confirm **taxable revenue**
- Output VAT arises from invoice issuance

### For Buyers
- Special invoices generate **input VAT**
- All valid invoices support **expense deduction** for CIT

## Practical Summary
- Invoices are transaction evidence, not tax coupons.
- VAT deduction depends on invoice type.
- CIT deduction depends on expense legitimacy.
