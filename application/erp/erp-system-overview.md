# ERP System Overview

## 1. What is an ERP System

**ERP (Enterprise Resource Planning)** is a business management system that integrates core business processes of an organization into a single unified platform.

It helps companies manage operations such as:

- Finance
- Procurement
- Inventory
- Sales
- Human Resources
- Manufacturing
- Supply Chain

The main goal of ERP is to **centralize data and streamline business workflows** across departments.

---

## 2. Why Companies Use ERP

Without ERP, departments often use separate systems:

Finance → accounting software  
Warehouse → inventory system  
Sales → CRM system  
HR → HR management tools

This leads to problems:

- Data duplication
- Inconsistent information
- Manual data transfer
- Poor visibility across departments

ERP solves this by providing **one integrated system and shared database**.

Example:

Sales order created → inventory updated → finance records revenue → shipment arranged automatically.

---

## 3. Typical ERP Users

ERP systems are mainly used by **internal business staff**.

Examples:

- Finance accountants
- Procurement officers
- Warehouse managers
- Sales managers
- HR staff
- Production planners
- Company executives

ERP is **not customer-facing software**.  
It is an **internal enterprise management system**.

---

## 4. Core ERP Modules

ERP systems are usually organized into modules.

### Finance Management

Handles company financial operations.

Typical features:

- General ledger
- Accounts payable
- Accounts receivable
- Financial reporting
- Budget management
- Tax management

---

### Procurement Management

Manages purchasing of goods and services.

Typical features:

- Purchase requisition
- Supplier management
- Purchase orders
- Goods receipt
- Invoice matching

---

### Inventory Management

Tracks stock and warehouse operations.

Typical features:

- Stock levels
- Warehouse locations
- Goods receipt
- Stock transfers
- Inventory adjustments
- Stock valuation

---

### Sales Management

Manages customer orders and sales processes.

Typical features:

- Sales orders
- Customer records
- Pricing management
- Order fulfillment
- Invoice generation

---

### Human Resource Management (HR)

Manages employee information.

Typical features:

- Employee records
- Payroll
- Leave management
- Recruitment
- Performance management

---

### Manufacturing / Production Management

Used by manufacturing companies.

Typical features:

- Bill of materials (BOM)
- Production planning
- Work orders
- Production scheduling
- Quality control

---

## 5. Example ERP Workflow

Example: Customer Order Fulfillment

1. Sales creates a **sales order**
2. System checks **inventory availability**
3. Warehouse prepares shipment
4. Finance generates **invoice**
5. Payment is recorded
6. Financial reports updated automatically

All steps happen within the **same ERP system**.

---

## 6. Key Characteristics of ERP

### Integrated Data

All modules share the same database.

Example:

Customer information used by:

- Sales
- Finance
- Support

---

### Process Automation

ERP automates business workflows.

Example:

Purchase order approved → supplier notified automatically.

---

### Real-time Visibility

Managers can see real-time business data:

- sales performance
- inventory levels
- financial status

---

### Standardized Processes

ERP helps companies follow consistent business processes.

## 8. ERP vs Other Enterprise Systems

| System Type | Purpose |
|-------------|--------|
| ERP | Internal enterprise operations |
| CRM | Customer relationship management |
| PAS | Insurance policy administration |
| SCM | Supply chain management |
| HRM | Human resource management |

ERP often integrates with these systems.

---

## 9. ERP System Architecture (Typical)

Modern ERP systems often follow this architecture:
```
Web / Mobile Interface
│
API Gateway
│
Application Services
│
Business Modules
(Finance / Inventory / HR / Sales)
│
Database
```


Many modern ERP systems are built using **microservices or modular architecture**.

---

## 10. Summary

ERP is the **core digital platform for enterprise operations**.

It integrates multiple business functions into a unified system, enabling:

- better efficiency
- data consistency
- business visibility
- standardized processes

ERP is widely used in industries such as:

- manufacturing
- retail
- logistics
- finance
- healthcare
- government
